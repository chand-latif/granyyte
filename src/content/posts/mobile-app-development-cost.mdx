"How much does an app cost?" is the first question every founder asks — and the one agencies answer most evasively. Here are the actual numbers, the factors that move them, and the traps that quietly double budgets.

## The short answer

In 2026, working with a quality agency:

- **Simple MVP** (one core flow, standard UI, basic backend): **$5,000–15,000**
- **Standard product** (auth, payments, chat, admin panel, integrations): **$15,000–40,000**
- **Complex platform** (marketplaces, real-time features, custom algorithms, multi-role systems): **$40,000–120,000+**

Big-city Western agencies often quote 2–4× these numbers for identical scope. Freelancer marketplaces quote less — with dramatically higher variance in what you actually receive.

## What actually drives the price

### 1. Number of screens and flows

An app with 10 screens is not half the work of an app with 20 — but screen count remains the best first-order predictor of cost. Every screen means design, logic, edge cases, and testing.

### 2. Backend complexity

A read-only content app needs almost no backend. A marketplace needs accounts, listings, search, payments, messaging, moderation, and notifications — the invisible half of your budget.

### 3. Third-party integrations

Stripe, Google Maps, Twilio, analytics, CRMs: each integration is usually days, not weeks. But *custom* integrations with legacy or poorly documented systems can consume entire sprints.

### 4. Cross-platform strategy

Building iOS and Android as separate native apps nearly doubles cost. One Flutter codebase covering both platforms is the single biggest lever for cutting budget without cutting scope — it's why it's our default stack.

### 5. Design ambition

Template-grade UI is cheap. Custom design systems with motion and polish add 15–30% — and are usually worth it for consumer products where first impressions decide retention.

## The costs nobody quotes

- **App store accounts:** $99/year (Apple) + $25 once (Google)
- **Infrastructure:** $0–50/month for most MVPs (Firebase/Supabase free tiers are generous), scaling with users
- **Maintenance:** budget 15–20% of build cost annually — OS updates, dependency upgrades, small fixes
- **The rebuild trap:** the most expensive app is the one built twice. Cutting corners on architecture at v1 is how $15k projects become $60k projects

## How to keep your budget sane

1. **Scope an MVP ruthlessly.** Your v1 needs one core loop done excellently — not twelve features done adequately.
2. **Choose cross-platform by default.** Go native only if you have a specific, named reason.
3. **Demand fixed pricing on scoped work.** Hourly billing on a poorly scoped project is a blank check.
4. **Ask who owns the code.** The answer should be an immediate "you do" — anything else is lock-in.

## What a real quote looks like

A serious agency will give you: a fixed price tied to a written scope, a week-by-week timeline, named deliverables, and clarity on what post-launch support costs. If you're getting a single number with no scope document, you're not getting a quote — you're getting an opening bid.
