"How much does an app cost?" is the first question every founder asks — and the one agencies answer most evasively. Here are the actual numbers, the factors that move them, and why building the smart way keeps the price far lower than you've been told.

## The short answer

Working with me in 2026, mobile app costs start from around **$500** and scale with what you actually need:

- **Simple app** (one core flow, standard UI, basic backend): **from ~$500**
- **Standard product** (auth, payments, chat, admin panel, a few integrations): **~$1,500–5,000**
- **Complex platform** (marketplaces, real-time features, custom logic, multi-role systems): **$5,000+**

Big-city Western agencies routinely quote 5–20× these numbers for identical scope. The difference isn't quality — it's overhead, and how the app is built.

## What actually drives the price

### 1. Number of screens and flows

An app with 10 screens isn't half the work of one with 20 — but screen count is still the best first-order predictor of cost. Every screen means design, logic, edge cases, and testing.

### 2. Backend complexity

A read-only content app needs almost no backend. A marketplace needs accounts, listings, search, payments, messaging, moderation, and notifications — the invisible half of the budget.

### 3. Third-party integrations

Stripe, Google Maps, push notifications, analytics: each is usually days, not weeks. But *custom* integrations with legacy or poorly documented systems can consume real time.

### 4. Cross-platform strategy

Building iOS and Android as separate native apps roughly triples cost. One Flutter codebase covering both is the single biggest lever for cutting budget without cutting scope — it's why it's my default.

### 5. Design ambition

Template-grade UI is cheap. A custom design system with motion and polish adds a bit — and is usually worth it for consumer products where first impressions decide retention.

## The costs nobody quotes

- **App store accounts:** $99/year (Apple) + $25 once (Google)
- **Infrastructure:** $0–50/month for most apps — Firebase and Supabase free tiers are generous, scaling with users
- **Maintenance:** budget 15–20% of build cost annually for OS updates, dependency upgrades, and small fixes
- **The rebuild trap:** the most expensive app is the one built twice. Cutting corners on architecture at v1 is how a cheap project becomes an expensive one

## How to keep your budget sane

1. **Scope an MVP ruthlessly.** Your v1 needs one core loop done excellently — not twelve features done adequately.
2. **Choose cross-platform by default.** Go native only if you have a specific, named reason.
3. **Demand fixed pricing on scoped work.** Hourly billing on a vague scope is a blank check.
4. **Ask who owns the code.** The answer should be an immediate "you do" — anything else is lock-in.

## What a real quote looks like

You should get: a fixed price tied to a written scope, a clear delivery timeline (mine is usually 2–4 weeks), named deliverables, and clarity on post-launch support. If you're handed a single number with no scope, that's not a quote — it's an opening bid.
